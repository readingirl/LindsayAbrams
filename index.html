I'm a digital-first journalist dedicated to changing the future of media through journalistic practice and human-centered design.

I can currently be found at Matter Ventures, where I got my start as a 2017 Google News Lab Fellow. In my new role, I run our media strategy and assist with the sourcing and training of early-stage, mission-driven media startups.

My pivot to media innovation from writing/editing began at New York University's Studio 20 program, where, as a Master's candidate, I worked with Vox.com's off-platform storytelling team in a project aimed at understanding how media organizations can prepare their newsrooms to impart valuable, trustworthy information in platform-native formats. You can read about my findings—where else—on Facebook.

Also at NYU, as the project manager for a collaboration with The Wall Street Journal, I lead a 12-person team in an effort to research, prototype, and test new ways to reach younger, less financially literate readers. I honed my video and web production skills through the creation of Finding Sanctuary, winner of the 2017 CPOY Award of Excellence for Online Multimedia Storytelling.

 

I'm always looking to be in touch with people working at the intersection of journalism, technology and design. If that's you, give me a shout on Twitter or shoot me an email.

I am a 2017 Google News Lab Fellow at Matter Ventures and a Master's candidate at New York University's Studio 20 program. I plan to graduate in 2017 ready to go forth and transform journalism.

Recent projects I was a part of include Finding Sanctuary, for which I was a web and video producer, and News Literacy 2017, for which I researched and reported on platform-native content.

You may have seen my earlier work at Salon, where I launched and lead the site's Sustainability vertical. As an assistant editor and then staff writer, I contributed daily with blog posts, editorials, reported pieces and author interviews.

Before that, I got my start as an editorial fellow at The Atlantic, where I contributed to, produced and edited for the site's Health vertical.

I also worked as a program coordinator for Journalism + Design at The New School, a Knight-funded interdisciplinary program merging design thinking with journalistic principles and practice.

I graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Wesleyan University, where I received my BA in cultural anthropology with a certificate in writing.
