Lindsay Abrams

Digital-First Journalist

I am an early-career journalist currently pursuing my Master's degree through New York University's Studio 20 program. I plan to graduate in December 2017 ready to go forth and transform journalism.

You may have seen my work at Salon, where I launched and lead the site's Sustainability vertical. As an assistant editor and then staff writer, I contributed daily with blog posts, editorials, reported pieces and author interviews.

Before that, I got my start as an editorial fellow at The Atlantic, where I contributed to, produced and edited for the site's Health vertical.

I also worked as a program coordinator for Journalism + Design at The New School, a Knight-funded interdisciplinary program merging design thinking with journalistic principles and practice.

I graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Wesleyan University, where I received my BA in cultural anthropology with a certificate in writing.

 
 

As you can probably imagine, I'm learning a ton of new things right now. Check back soon for new surprises.
